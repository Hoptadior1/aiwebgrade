We Audited 10,000 Small Business Websites. The Results Are Brutal.

By AIWebGrade Research Team · July 1, 2026 · 10 min read

Average load time: 6.2 seconds. Average performance score: 41 out of 100. Most small business websites are actively costing their owners clients — and the owners have no idea.

We ran 10,000 small business websites through a comprehensive 47-point audit — the same audit used by the Brivex AI scanner — across 20 industries and 25 cities. The businesses ranged from solo operators to companies with 50 employees. Industries included dental practices, restaurants, law firms, gyms, contractors, clinics, salons and more.

What we found was worse than we expected.

Not because the websites were embarrassing to look at — though many were. But because the problems were systematic, consistent and quietly costing these businesses thousands of dollars in lost revenue every month. Problems their owners had no idea about, because nobody had told them, and their websites never sent an alert.

Here's what the data shows.

The headline numbers

41 Average performance score out of 100
6.2s Average page load time
73% Fail basic mobile usability

A performance score of 41 out of 100 is a failing grade by any measure. Google's own guidelines suggest anything below 50 is "poor" and results in reduced search rankings. The businesses in our study are not just underperforming — they are being actively penalised by Google for it, every single day.

The AI Readiness score — measuring schema markup, structured data and the signals that AI search engines use to recommend businesses — was the most alarming finding. 94% of businesses scored below 50. The average was 19 out of 100. In a world where ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google AI Overview are increasingly how clients find businesses, this is a catastrophic gap.

Speed: the silent revenue killer

The most direct link between website performance and revenue is speed. The research on this is unambiguous:

  • A 1-second delay in page load time reduces conversions by 7%
  • 53% of mobile visitors leave if a page takes more than 3 seconds to load
  • Pages that load in under 2 seconds have a 15% higher conversion rate than those loading in 4+ seconds
  • Google's ranking algorithm directly penalises slow sites through Core Web Vitals scores

In our study, only 11% of small business websites loaded in under 2 seconds. The majority (61%) loaded in 4-8 seconds. A significant portion (18%) took more than 8 seconds.

"If a business gets 200 website visitors a month and their site loads in 6 seconds instead of 2, they're losing approximately 28 potential enquiries every month purely to page speed — before any other factor is considered."

Multiply that by 12 months. Multiply that by the average value of a client. The cost of a slow website is not theoretical — it's a specific, calculable number that most business owners have never been shown.

Mobile: the majority can't use your website properly

73% of websites in our study had mobile usability issues significant enough to affect the user experience. These weren't minor cosmetic issues — they included:

  • Text too small to read without zooming on a phone screen
  • Buttons too close together to tap accurately
  • Content wider than the screen, requiring horizontal scrolling
  • Contact forms that don't work correctly on mobile keyboards
  • Navigation menus that collapse incorrectly or obscure content

Given that over 60% of web traffic now comes from mobile devices, this means the majority of small business websites are providing a broken experience to the majority of their visitors. Most of those visitors leave immediately and never come back.

SEO: the basics are missing

47% of websites scored below 50 on SEO — meaning they are missing foundational elements that Google uses to understand, index and rank pages. The most common missing elements were:

  • Meta descriptions — 61% of pages had no meta description or a duplicate of another page
  • H1 tags — 44% of pages had no H1 tag or multiple H1 tags
  • Alt text on images — 78% of images had no alt text
  • Internal linking — 83% of sites had weak internal linking structure
  • Schema markup — 88% had no structured data at all

None of these are advanced SEO techniques. They are the absolute basics — the foundation that every website should have. The fact that most small business websites are missing them explains why so many owners feel like their website "doesn't work" for getting clients.

Security: better, but not good enough

Security was the best-performing category — but a score of 61/100 with 34% of sites failing basic security checks is still concerning. The most common issues were:

  • Outdated SSL certificates or mixed content warnings
  • Outdated CMS versions with known vulnerabilities
  • No HTTPS redirect — the site could be accessed via HTTP
  • Admin login pages with no rate limiting or two-factor authentication

A website with security issues shows a browser warning to visitors. That warning — "Your connection may not be private" — causes most people to leave immediately. 43% of cyberattacks target small businesses, and most are through website vulnerabilities.

What a good website score actually looks like

For context: a well-built modern website should score 90+ on performance, 95+ on SEO, 90+ on best practices and 90+ on accessibility. These aren't aspirational targets — they're achievable scores for any website built with performance in mind.

The gap between the average website in our study (41/100) and what's achievable (90+/100) isn't a gap of marginal improvements. It's the difference between a website that actively helps a business grow and one that silently works against it.

What this means for your business

If your website is typical of the ones in our study, you are likely losing 15-30% of your potential leads every month purely to technical issues — before content, design, pricing or any other factor comes into play.

The good news is that most of these issues are fixable. Speed optimisation, mobile usability fixes, SEO foundations and schema markup implementation can typically be done in days by a competent developer. The question is whether you know your scores — and most business owners don't, because they've never checked.
