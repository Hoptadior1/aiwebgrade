Hotel Direct Bookings vs OTA Commission: The Real Math

By AIWebGrade Team·July 12, 2026·8 min read

Every booking through Booking.com or Expedia hands over 15-25% before you've covered a single cost. Here's what shifting even a fraction of that to direct bookings is actually worth.

OTA commission isn't a small line item — it's often one of the largest recurring costs a property has, quietly taken off the top of every single booking. The good news: you don't need to eliminate OTAs to make a real dent. Shifting even 15-20% of bookings from OTA to direct changes the numbers significantly.

15-25%typical OTA commission per booking
0%commission on a booking made directly through your own site

The actual math on a shift toward direct

A property doing 1,000 room-nights a month at a $150 average rate through OTAs at 20% commission is paying $30,000/month in fees. Shift just 200 of those room-nights to direct booking and you keep an extra $6,000/month — without raising rates or adding a single new guest. That's the leverage available before you even think about growing overall demand.

Why guests still book through OTAs even when they know your site exists

Often it's simple habit and trust — OTAs feel like a safe default, and guests don't have a strong reason to seek out the direct alternative unless one is made obvious. This is the opportunity: most guests aren't loyal to the OTA, they just haven't been given a clear reason to book anywhere else.

What actually shifts bookings toward direct

Visible rate parity or a direct-booking advantage

If your direct rate matches or beats the OTA rate, say so clearly on your site. Even better: offer something the OTA can't — free breakfast, a room upgrade, flexible cancellation, or a small credit — so choosing direct feels like the obviously smarter choice, not just an equal one.

A booking flow that's actually as easy as the OTA's

Many hotel direct booking engines are clunky compared to a polished OTA interface. If your own booking flow takes longer or feels less trustworthy than Booking.com's, guests will default back to the familiar option even after finding your site. A quick way to see where the friction actually sits is running your own booking page through Brivex AI's scanner the same way a guest experiences it, not the way you assume it works from the admin side.

Capturing guests who already found you through an OTA

A guest browsing your OTA listing who then Googles your hotel name directly is showing clear intent to book with you specifically. Your website needs to capture that moment — clear rates, an easy direct booking path, and a visible reason to skip the OTA fee entirely. A full direct-booking growth plan ties this moment together with the retention side too, since a guest you win back directly once is far more likely to skip the OTA the second time.

📊 What a modest shift is worth over a year

  • 1,000 monthly room-nights, shifting 20% from OTA (20% commission) to direct (0% commission)
  • At a $150 average rate, that's $6,000/month recovered — $72,000/year
  • This is pure margin recovery, not new revenue that required new demand generation

"OTAs aren't the enemy — they're a genuinely useful discovery channel. The properties winning on margin are the ones that capture the guest again once that guest already knows who they are."

⚠️ Don't undercut your own rate parity agreements. Many OTA contracts require rate parity — check yours before offering a lower direct rate, and lean on non-price incentives like free breakfast or flexible cancellation instead if parity is required.

Common questions

Should a hotel try to eliminate OTA bookings entirely?

No — OTAs bring genuine discovery traffic a hotel's own site can't reach alone, especially for new guests unfamiliar with the property. The goal is shifting the balance toward more direct bookings for returning and price-sensitive guests, not eliminating OTAs completely.

Is a price match guarantee against OTAs actually effective?

Yes, when paired with a visible reason to book direct beyond price — free breakfast, flexible cancellation, or a room upgrade. Price parity alone often isn't enough of a nudge; guests need an additional reason once price is no longer the deciding factor.

How long does it take to see a meaningful shift toward direct bookings?

Website and offer changes can show results within weeks for guests already considering the property. Building broader direct-booking habit among past guests through loyalty programs and email typically takes several months to fully take hold.
