The Tutor Website Guide: Matching the Exact Search a Parent Is Running

By AIWebGrade Team·July 8, 2026·7 min read

80% of parents research and compare tutors online before booking a session, usually searching for a very specific combination of subject, level and sometimes exam board — not a general tutoring service.

Parents searching for a tutor are rarely looking generally. They're searching for something specific — GCSE maths, A-level chemistry, a particular exam board's syllabus, help with a specific learning difficulty. A tutoring website that only presents "we tutor all subjects" fails to match this precise search intent, even when the tutor genuinely does cover the subject in question.

This mismatch matters because a parent comparing several tutors simultaneously will gravitate toward whichever site immediately confirms the exact match they're searching for, rather than digging through a generalist page to confirm whether their specific need is actually covered.

What actually matches the specific search

Dedicated pages for each subject and level combination

Rather than one general page, separate content for "GCSE Maths tutoring" and "A-Level Chemistry tutoring" matches the specific searches parents actually run, and performs better in search results for those exact terms than a single combined page ever could.

Qualifications tied directly to the specific subject

A parent searching for chemistry help wants to see chemistry-specific qualifications and experience, not a general statement of teaching ability. Specificity here builds far more confidence than broad claims of subject versatility.

Clear information about exam board experience where relevant

For subjects with multiple exam board variations, stating experience with the specific board a student is following removes an important point of uncertainty for parents trying to match tutor expertise to their child's actual curriculum.

"A parent isn't searching for 'a tutor' — they're searching for 'a GCSE maths tutor near me who understands the AQA syllabus.' The tutor whose site confirms that exact match fastest usually gets the enquiry."

Trial session offers reduce a common hesitation

Many parents are uncertain whether a specific tutor will be a good fit for their child. A clearly stated trial or introductory session option reduces the risk of committing to an ongoing arrangement before knowing whether the tutor and student work well together.

Special educational needs experience deserves clear mention

Parents of children with specific learning needs often search with that requirement explicitly in mind, and stating relevant experience clearly can be the deciding factor for this particular, often underserved, segment of enquiries.

A quick self-check

Search for a specific subject and level you teach, plus your city, and see whether your site surfaces content matching that exact combination — or only a general "we tutor everything" page that requires a parent to dig further to confirm the match.

What good actually looks like

A well-built tutoring website has dedicated pages for major subject and level combinations, states exam board experience where relevant, and offers a clear trial session — matching the precise, high-intent search parents actually run rather than a generic services page.

Group sessions need separate positioning from one-on-one

If both formats are offered, describing them separately helps parents understand the different value proposition — group sessions for cost-conscious families, one-on-one for fully personalised attention.

Common questions

Should hourly rates be published?

Yes, generally — parents comparing multiple tutors want to quickly gauge affordability, and hidden pricing is a common reason a parent moves to the next search result without ever making contact.

Is online or in-person tutoring information more important to state clearly?

Both should be stated clearly if offered, since this is often a deciding factor for parents with specific scheduling or logistical constraints comparing multiple tutors simultaneously.

How important are parent testimonials versus student results?

Both matter — parent testimonials address trust and communication, while specific grade improvements or exam results address the tangible outcome parents are ultimately paying for.
